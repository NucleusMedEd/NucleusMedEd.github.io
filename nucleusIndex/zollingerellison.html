Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome

A 44 year-old female presents to the emergency department with burning epigastric pain that radiates to the right upper quadrant. Review of symptoms is positive for nausea, vomiting, food intolerance and watery diarrhea. She also reports a 10 pound weight loss. She states she experienced this pain prior and it was treated with antibiotics. She is admitted to the hospital and during her admission she develops one episode of hematemesis. A CT scan of the abdomen is obtained and reveals a lesion in the pancreas and the left lobe of the liver.

▹ What is the most likely diagnosis?

A. Cholecystitis

B. gastroesophageal syndrome

C. primary biliary cirrhosis

D. Stable angina

E. Zollinger-Ellison syndrome

Answer:

▹ Mechanism for the underlying condition is?

A. acid hypersecretion

B. autoimmune process

C. bacterial infection

D. iatrogenic

E. lifestyle factors

Answer: Acid hypersecretion

A gastrinoma is a gastrin-secreting tumor that occurs in the pancreas and/or duodenum. The tumor cells produce large amounts of gastrin which leads to hyperplasia of parietal cells in the fundus and increased basal acid secretion. As a result, multiple ulcerations develop in the stomach and may extend into the duodenum. The tumor also causes the release of secretin and bicarbonate which is responsible for diarrhea. Excessive diarrhea leads to malabsorption.

▹ What syndrome is associated with this patients diagnosis?

A. Sipple's syndrome

B. Tuberous sclerosis

C. VIPoma

D. Von Recklinghausen's disease

E. Wermer's syndrome

Answer: Wermer's syndrome
Multiple endocrine neoplasia type 1 (MEN 1), also called Wermer's syndrome, is an autosomal dominant familial syndrome characterized by hyperparathyroidism, pancreatic endocrine tumors and pituitary tumors. Symptoms of hyperparathyroidism are typically the presenting features of MEN1, however some present with the signs of peptic ulcer disease as seen in Zollinger-Ellison syndrome. Many forms of MEN1 can be apparent depending on the endocrine organs involved and the hormones secreted. Therefore, there is variation in the clinical presentation of a patient with MEN1.

▹ The most initial step in diagnosis is?

A. basal acid output

B. fasting gastrin level

C. secretin stimulation test

D. somatostatin receptor scintigraphy

E. urea breath test

Answer: fasting gastrin level
A fasting serum gastrin level is used to screen for Zollinger-Ellison syndrome (ZES). It is the most sensitive and specific method to establish a diagnosis of ZES. Screening criteria includes patients with multiple lesions, ulcers distal to the duodenal bulb, and those that are refractory to medical therapy. Individuals with a strong family history or those who present with diarrhea, steatorrhea or weight loss should also be screened. Three fasting levels of gastrin must be obtained on different days. A gastrin level greater than 1000 pg/mL in the absence of achlorhydria is diagnostic of a gastrinoma.
