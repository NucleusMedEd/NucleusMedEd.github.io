Side Effects

Hydrochlorothiazide

This patient is having an acute gout flare due to the addition of hydrochlorothiazide. Thiazide diuretics aggravate gout by competitively inhibiting the secretion of uric acid. They lead to hyperuricemia and are contraindicated when gout is present in the patient history. Other side effects of thiazides which are noteworthy include, hyperglycemia, hypercalcemia (therefore can be used in treatment of nephrolithiasis by decreasing calcium concentrations in renal tubules), and hypokalemia(although to a lesser degree than loop diuretics).
