<p id="zollingerEllison_overview">Zollinger Ellison Syndrome (ZES) is caused by a non-beta islet, gastrin secreting tumor of the pancreas. This tumor stimulates the acid-secreting cells of the stomach causing gastrointestinal mucosal ulceration. Primary ulcerations, severe gastric acid hypersecretion,&nbsp;and the presence of a non-beta islet cell tumor of the pancreas or duodenum are diagnostic.&nbsp;The duodenum, stomach and/or jejunum are typically affected.&nbsp;One percent of patients with peptic ulcer disease present with ZES. In ZES, parietal cell mass is increased, which leads to further impairment of mucosal defenses. As a result of gastric hypersecretion and inactive pancreatic enzymes, diarrhea and malabsorption in patients with gastrinomas is common.&nbsp;Other symptoms include abdominal pain and heartburn.</p>
